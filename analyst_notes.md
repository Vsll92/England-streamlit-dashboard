# Analyst notes: England offensive behavior

- England produced 10.51 xG from 81 shots across 7 matches, with 10.8% of possessions ending in a shot.
- Build-up was almost evenly split between short (51.1%) and long/direct (48.9%) starts.
- The first build-up progression most often targeted the right lane (39.3%), ahead of the left (32.8%) and center (27.9%).
- Early build-up receptions went mainly to midfielders (28.3%) and forwards (24.9%), with centre-backs still heavily involved (20.1%).
- England reached the final third in 58.5% of possessions and created 293 total entries (41.9 per match).
- Final-third entries were right-lane dominant (49.5%) and were driven mainly by short passes (61.1%) plus a sizeable direct/deep-pass share (30.0%).
- After first Z3 entry, England entered the box in 60.2% of cases and created a shot in 21.0%.
- Zone 14 remained important: 141 entries into Z14, 109 receptions there, and 39 shots in possessions containing Z14 involvement.
- Harry Kane led England shot volume and xG (Harry Kane: 3.05 xG), while Kieran Trippier, Bukayo Saka, Phil Foden and Jude Bellingham were among the strongest chance creators by key passes and xA.
- Chance creation was driven mostly by set-piece situations by shot volume (53.1%), but carry/dribble actions supplied the highest open-play xG share in the derived source model.
